# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Individuals and small teams who need to decide whether a suspicious email is genuine before they send money, change payment details, enter credentials, or open a risky link. Primary business users include bookkeepers, realtors, small law offices, agencies, family offices, and executive assistants working in Google Workspace or Microsoft 365 without a dedicated security team.

## Product Purpose

Message Loupe gives non-technical users a private, plain-English second opinion on suspicious email. It analyzes the original message in the browser, explains sender, routing, authentication, content, attachment, and link signals, and requires outside verification for high-risk requests. Success means helping a user pause before an expensive mistake without uploading or storing the email.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, and candid. The product should reduce uncertainty without fear-based language, explain evidence without security jargon, and state its limits as clearly as its findings.

## Anti-references

Do not resemble an enterprise security dashboard, a fear-driven antivirus sales page, or a generic AI product landing page. Avoid claims of certainty, exaggerated threat imagery, dark-pattern urgency, decorative cyber motifs, and vague promises that the product can prevent every scam.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with the decision the user needs to make, then show the evidence.
2. Treat privacy as a verifiable product behavior, not a slogan.
3. Use plain language while preserving the technical distinction between authentic delivery and safe intent.
4. Make the safest next action obvious for money, credential, and account-change requests.
5. Keep the scanner usable without requiring an account, installation, or security expertise.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Meet WCAG 2.2 AA for contrast, keyboard operation, focus visibility, semantics, and reduced motion. Never rely on color alone for verdicts. Use direct language that works for people under time pressure and for users unfamiliar with email-security terminology.
